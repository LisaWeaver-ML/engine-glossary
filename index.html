Consumer Journey
🔽
Engine Consumer Funnel — End-to-End Flow
5 core business processes from lead entry to revenue recognition · Source: Confluence MABI
1
Consumer Arrives (Entry)
Consumer enters via a supply integration: partner page, embedded widget, API/SDK, or affiliate link. Engine checks for an existing cookie (valid 12 months for partner pages).
BP1: Lead Generation
2
Lead Created — Lead UUID Assigned
A unique Lead UUID is generated and becomes the system-wide join key for all downstream events. The lead is tied permanently to the originating supply subaccount. Same person + different partner = different Lead UUID.
BP1: Lead Generation
3
Application Submitted → Rate Table Created
"Submitted application" is a derived concept — proxied by rate table creation. Consumer has filled in required fields. One lead can generate multiple rate tables if they re-submit with changed inputs.
BP2: Offer Decisioning
4
Offer Decisioning — Demand API Calls
Engine calls dynamic lenders' APIs and applies the Demand API Filter (DAF). Offers are scored using rec_score = pred_converted × payout × EES. Lenders are ranked by their best offer (anchor); within a lender, offers are sorted by borrower fit (gap to requested amount).
BP2: Offer Decisioning
5
Rate Table Displayed to Consumer
Consumer sees pre-approved offers sorted by rec score (highest = top position). Static/special offers may also be shown. 2LM consumers only see a special offer if declined.
BP2: Offer Decisioning
6
Offer Click
Consumer clicks one or more offers. Every click is recorded — including duplicates. For CPC contracts, revenue is calculated at click time (no EDE required). Click ID joins to LFLI for CPC contracts.
BP3: Offer Clicks
7
Lender Decision (Off-Platform)
Consumer is on the lender's site. Engine does not track what happens here. Lenders perform their own underwriting. If approved and funded, the lender later reports a conversion event back to Engine via an EDE file.
BP4: Funding Approval & EDE
8
Conversion Event Received
Engine receives an External Demand Event (EDE) file from the lender (or captures the CPC click). This is stored in external_demand_events. 99.7% of monetizations have an associated click event.
BP4: Funding Approval & EDE
9
Revenue & Payout Calculated
LFLI Calculator applies the active demand contract (CPL, CPA, CPC, RevShare, or Funded) to compute Engine's revenue. Then applies the supply contract to compute what Engine owes the supply partner. Both written to lead_financial_line_items.
BP5: Financial Reconciliation
✓
Reporting: Attributed vs. Booked
Attributed = assigned at lead entry time. Booked = revenue recognized when loan funds (can lag days to weeks). Looker's Lead by Demand table reads from LFLI. BBH (Booked by Hour) is the Finance invoicing source of truth.
BP5: Financial Reconciliation

🤝
Double Opt-In Model
Both supply and demand partners must independently agree before a consumer sees an offer
Supply Partner Opts In
1. Configure SSA
Supply partner sets up a Supply Sub-Account for their traffic source (vertical, integration type, commercial terms).
↓
2. Create Supply Enablement
Supply partner (or Engine PS) links the SSA to specific DSAs — explicitly choosing whose offers appear on their traffic.

Example: SuperMoney enables SoFi's personal loan DSA but not LendingClub's.
+
Demand Partner Opts In
1. Configure DSA
Demand partner sets up a Demand Sub-Account for a product line (personal loans, credit cards, etc.).
↓
2. Build Offer Catalog
Demand partner defines which products they're willing to show: rate tables, eligibility rules (min score, state restrictions), offer display, and contract terms.

Example: SoFi publishes personal loan offers for 680+ credit, $5k–$100k.
↓
Engine Matches
When a consumer arrives via the SSA, Engine checks only the DSAs that SSA is enabled for. Within each enabled DSA, it checks whether the consumer meets the Offer Catalog eligibility rules. Offers are ranked by rec_score and returned.
↓
Consumer Sees Offer
A consumer only sees an offer when both conditions are met: the supply partner has enabled that DSA, and the demand partner's catalog includes an offer the consumer qualifies for. Neither side is forced into a match.

Commercial Model

Contract Types — Revenue & Payout Triggers
How Engine earns revenue and pays supply partners · Source: Confluence MABI BP5
Type Trigger Event Revenue Formula
CPC Consumer clicks an offer on the rate table Flat fee per first click. Calculated at click time — no EDE needed.
CPL Lead submitted / rate table created (qualified lead) Flat fee per qualified lead. Common as supply payout contract.
CPA Lender-defined action (e.g., approval, account open) Flat fee per approved action. Common for credit cards & savings.
RevShare Loan funded — lender reports funded amount in EDE file % of funded amount (e.g., 60%). Most common for First Look loans. ~80% of supply partners on RevShare.
Funded Loan or account confirmed funded Flat fee per funded event. Similar to CPA but specifically tied to funding confirmation.
⚠️ Revenue in LFLI can change retroactively (up to 90 days) if contracts are backdated. Always use current LFLI figures — not historical snapshots.

How money moves from consumer → demand partner → Engine → supply partner
CPC Contract

↓

↓

EDE required. Revenue lags by days–weeks. LFLI + BBH for reconciliation.
Funded Contract
Consumer clicks → applies → loan fully funded by lender
↓
Demand partner confirms funding via EDE with actual funded amount
↓
Engine earns fee based on funded loan amount. Highest revenue potential per event.
↓
Longest lag (funding can take weeks). CTF % (Click-to-Fund) is the key conversion metric.

Ranking & Pricing
🏆
Rec Score Formula & Rate Table Ordering
PS Override v3 · Source: Confluence Data Science
rec_score = pred_converted × payout × EES
Units: cents · Higher score = higher position on rate table
pred_converted
Model's predicted probability consumer will convert
payout
What Engine earns if loan converts (in dollars)
EES
Expected Earning Share. 0.6 = neutral baseline
PS Override v3 Ranking Steps
1
Score every offer: rec_score = pred × payout × EES
2
Find each lender's anchor: max(rec_score) across all of that lender's offers
3
Order lenders by anchor (highest first). Within a lender, order offers by gap to requested amount (smallest gap = top)
4
Adjusted scores step down 1% per rank from the rank-1 rec_score: adj_k = rec₁ × (1 − (k−1) × 0.01)
5
Back-calculate returned_pred = adj_score / (payout × EES) so upstream multiplication recovers the intended order
📄 Source: PS Override — Rank-Calibration Mechanics (Confluence Data Science)
🎚️
EESO — Expected Earning Share Override
How EESO shifts rec score and rate table position
EESO overrides the EES multiplier in the rec score formula. The neutral point (0.6) neither boosts nor suppresses. Values above 0.6 push offers up; below 0.6 push them down.
0.0 — Suppress 0.6 — Neutral 1.0+ — Boost
↓
Below 0.6
Rec score suppressed
Offer ranks lower
Payout adjusted down
◆
= 0.6
Neutral baseline
No impact on rank
Standard payout
↑
Above 0.6
Rec score boosted
Offer ranks higher
Payout adjusted up
Price Ceiling — a cap applied alongside EESO to limit how high rec score economics can go.
RTP partners — supply payout = rec_score ÷ 100 per clicked offer.

Step 3 — Consumer enters the full Engine marketplace form
The calculator CTA hands the consumer into the standard Engine marketplace form. For calculators that pre-fill data (loan purpose, amount), those fields are skipped. Consumer completes remaining fields, then proceeds through Engine's normal decisioning pipeline → offer wall → Apply Now.

📥
1LM: First Look Marketplace
Three integration paths — Native API, Spark Fully Hosted, and Spark Filtering — each with its own flow. Tab between them to compare.
Engine POSTs consumer data to the partner's own underwriting endpoint in real time. The partner runs their model and returns an offer or decline. Must respond within 10 seconds.
Partner uploads their model to Black Box (spark.engine.com). Engine runs leads through it internally — no external API call is made. The partner never receives individual lead requests.
Spark pre-screens the lead in Black Box against the partner's criteria. Only leads that pass are forwarded to the partner's own underwriting API — eliminating unnecessary calls for leads that would be declined.
📥
2LM: Second Look Marketplace
Triggered when all 1LM lenders decline. 2LM partners bid on a CPC basis for access to the consumer. No real-time underwriting API required. Revenue is captured at the click, not at funding.
